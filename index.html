The Hidden Cost of Cheap Protein

Fast food isn't a choice problem. It's an infrastructure problem. The sodium penalty is highest where options are fewest.

In neighborhoods where a Popeyes or McDonald's is the closest grocery-adjacent option, fast food isn't fast food — it's infrastructure. The sodium load in these meals isn't accidental. It's the result of decades of formulation decisions, supply chain economics, and marketing targeted at communities with the fewest alternatives.

Not all chains are equal. The gap between the worst and best options within the same price tier is often larger than expected — and it's not random. It tracks closely with who the chain's primary customer is assumed to be.

How to read this: Average sodium across sampled menu items. The "Best Option" is the lowest-sodium item that still constitutes a meal — not a side salad with no dressing.

The pattern: Chains that market heavily to lower-income zip codes average 340mg more sodium per item than chains that position as "premium fast casual." Same industry. Different customers. Different formula.

The same item. The same brand. Formulated differently by country. The US consistently leads in sodium — not because American taste preferences demand it, but because US food policy allows it.

The formula was always flexible. The UK's soft drink sugar tax (2018) prompted reformulation across dozens of products within 18 months. The US has no equivalent sodium policy for fast food. The difference on your tray isn't taste — it's regulation.

Too Old, Too Paid

A congressional salary of $174,000 is more than three times the US median household income. It hasn't changed since 2009. Meanwhile the federal minimum wage hasn't changed since 2009 either — but the gap between them has never been more visible. This project maps that gap alongside the demographic reality of who's making these decisions.

Note on framing: This project doesn't argue congressional pay is too high or too low. It contextualizes it against wages most Americans actually earn — and lets you filter by your own state.
