Protein Efficiency

How much protein are you
actually buying?

Fast food is one of the most efficient ways to buy protein in America — if you ignore the sodium. This tool shows you the real exchange rate: grams of protein per dollar, and milligrams of sodium per gram of protein.

Efficiency protein (g) ÷ price ($)
·
Sodium penalty sodium (mg) ÷ protein (g)
·
Prices 2025–26 national averages
How to read this: Efficiency (g/$) tells you how much protein you get per dollar spent. Sodium penalty (mg/g) tells you how much sodium you pay per gram of protein. High efficiency + low penalty = the best deal. High efficiency + high penalty = the protein trap. Example: Chick-fil-A 12pc Grilled Nuggets → 38g protein ÷ $6.49 = 5.9 g/$ · 660mg ÷ 38g = 17 mg sodium per gram of protein
