## CONTENT MODES

Tags describe subject. Content mode describes the ask of the reader. Every piece has one primary content mode.

**Action mode** — The piece asks the reader to do something: contact a representative, share the data, change a behavior, apply pressure. Civic and Food Systems pieces typically land here.

**Education mode** — The piece asks the reader to know something and feel something. No external action required. Cultural and Identity pieces typically land here. Obsidian Futures label is most common here.

**Tool mode** — The piece gives the reader something to use repeatedly: a calculator, a lookup, a personal data tool. The ask is repeated engagement. FrameShift, Specialist Map, and Forever Loved are tool mode.

## OBSIDIAN FUTURES LABEL

Applied to pieces in Education mode that center Black cultural experience. Not a design system trigger on its own — AMA approves the dark treatment per piece. The label appears on the hub card and piece page as a tag, not a separate brand identifier.

## MULTI-TAG RULES

- Every piece has at least one tag
- No piece has more than four tags
- Tags are listed in order of editorial weight — the primary argument tag comes first
- Tags appear on both the hub card and the piece page in the same order
