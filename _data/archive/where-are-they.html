Where Are They?

White wealth is framed as voluntary warmth. Black wealth is treated as obligatory debt. This tool makes the architecture visible.

The Question No One's Asking

Every disaster produces the same scene: a Black artist is tagged on social media before she's checked if her family is safe. The man who owns the team in her hometown — worth 11× her net worth — is not mentioned.

This isn't random. It's architecture.

75% Of celebrities Kevin Hart called out for Harvey 2017 were Black — while Houston billionaires worth 11× more were never named

What This Piece Reveals

  • Government abdication — Why are private individuals asked at all?
  • Same mechanism, different reaction — Dolly Parton and Oprah Winfrey launched identical fund structures. Only one faced conspiracy theories.
  • The Black Tax at scale — The structural expectation that visible Black wealth compensate for government failure.
  • Domicile = obligation — If you chose Florida's no-income-tax structure, you chose Florida's disasters. Yet Florida billionaires are never asked.
  • Owners vs. players — NFL and NBA athletes are called out. The team owners who profit from their labor are not.

Methodology

What This Tool Measures

This tool tracks public accountability discourse — who is publicly called upon to give during disasters, and who is not. It does not track private giving, which is unknowable.

The tool uses two inclusion criteria:

  • Domicile state — Primary residence as documented by Forbes, tax records, or property records
  • Birth state — State of origin, which often triggers public expectation of connection

Net worth figures are from Forbes 2025 for consistency. "Asked" status is documented through news coverage, social media call-outs, or viral campaigns.

The Black Tax — Academic Framing

The concept of "Black Tax" comes from peer-reviewed research. The University of Minnesota Gender Policy Report defines it as the structural expectation that Black families compensate for government neglect:

"Black families literally do the work of nation states in building a robust social safety net for their communities... The Black Tax is both a hallmark of traditional racial solidarity and evidence of government neglect."
University of Minnesota Gender Policy Report

This tool argues that celebrity disaster accountability discourse is the Black Tax operating at scale — visible Black wealth is expected to compensate for structural failure, while white wealth of equal or greater magnitude is not.
