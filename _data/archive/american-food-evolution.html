The same food isn't the same

How the ingredients in common American foods quietly transformed from 1960 to 2020 — and what the research says it's doing to us.

The hypothesis: Saying "I ate pizza every day in college and I turned out fine" means something fundamentally different depending on when you went to college. A boomer's college pizza (~1965) had ~8 whole ingredients. A Gen Z student's frozen pizza (~2020) has 25–35 ingredients, many synthetic. The NIH proved this distinction matters: ultra-processed diets cause ~500 extra calories/day and weight gain, even when matched for nutrition.
