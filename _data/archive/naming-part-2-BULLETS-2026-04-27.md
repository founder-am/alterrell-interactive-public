- The actress (Queen Latifah) was already culturally visible before the show. The character created a separate, distinct naming event.

## TAB 4 — FRESH PRINCE [IN PROGRESS]

- [NON-ANALYTICAL BRIDGE NEEDED — Will Smith as a cultural phenomenon at that moment. The show was enormous. The names moved in specific, counter-intuitive ways. Open with the show's cultural weight before the surprising data.]
- Carlton Banks: declining through the run. The character was beloved as a comedic foil — funny, not aspirational. The community did not want to name a child after the joke. Character identity mechanism failed.
- Alfonso Ribeiro: 2× increase from baseline. The actor's name moved slightly — actor identity mechanism at minimum signal. Small but directional.
- The contrast: Whitley 390× (character identity, high aspiration) vs. Carlton declining (character identity, zero aspiration in the role). Same mechanism. Cultural coding of the character determines whether it fires.
- Tatyana Ali (Ashley Banks): the name Tatyana moved 111× from baseline — 1 in 215 Black baby girls at 1999 peak. Not the character name Ashley — too common to track. The actress's rare, specific name moved. Actor identity mechanism at high scale.
- The Fresh Prince gives two clean actor-identity data points (Tatyana 111×, Alfonso 2×) and one character-identity failure (Carlton declining) in one show. The variation inside one series is the argument.
