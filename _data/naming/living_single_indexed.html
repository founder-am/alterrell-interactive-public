LIVING SINGLE

Three Names, One Show • 1993–1998

How to Read This Chart: All names are indexed to 1993 (show debut year) = 100. This shows relative growth, not absolute numbers. A value of 300 means the name tripled from its 1993 baseline.

Living Single premiered in 1993 on Fox, following six Black friends in Brooklyn. The show became a cultural phenomenon, running five seasons and establishing itself as one of the defining Black sitcoms of the 1990s.

What makes this case fascinating: all three names grew together. Khadijah (a Muslim/Arabic name), Regine (French), and Latifah (Queen Latifah's stage name, also Arabic) all spiked around the show's debut—showing how a beloved show legitimizes multiple ethnic names simultaneously within the Black community.

Unlike crossover hits that reach mainstream white audiences, Living Single created "Black authority"—these names became popular primarily among Black families. The chart shows moderate absolute numbers but dramatic relative growth (3-4× increases), demonstrating cultural legitimacy within the community.
