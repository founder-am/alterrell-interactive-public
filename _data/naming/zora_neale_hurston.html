Posthumous Canonization

Zora Neale Hurston died in 1960, largely forgotten. "Their Eyes Were Watching God" was out of print. Then in 1975, Alice Walker literally searched for Hurston's grave in an overgrown Florida cemetery and placed a headstone. The essay "In Search of Zora Neale Hurston" sparked a revival.

The name stayed rare through the 1990s (avg 11 babies/year). But the 2000s-2010s explosion came from curriculum adoption: high school AP English, college courses, book clubs. If you're Black and educated in America after 2000, you've likely read Zora.

2018: "Barracoon" (her final book) published posthumously after 87 years in archives. More media attention, more conversations. 2019: Peak at 349 babies. This is what happens when literature becomes required reading 40+ years after death.
