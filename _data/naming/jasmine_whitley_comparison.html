A Different World • 1987–1993

Jasmine Guy (actress, exotic floral name) played Whitley Gilbert (character, Southern aristocratic name)

Jasmine Guy brought Whitley Gilbert to life on A Different World from 1987 to 1993. The Cosby Show spinoff followed students at fictional HBCU Hillman College, with Whitley as the bougie Southern belle who became one of TV's most beloved Black female characters.

Here's where it gets extraordinary: Before the show, the name Whitley was given to an average of just 1.3 babies per year. It was virtually nonexistent. By 1988, it exploded to 508—a 390× increase. An upper-class white Southern name became acceptable for Black families overnight.

Meanwhile, Jasmine was already moderately popular (656 average pre-show) but skyrocketed to 12,063 in 1993—an 18× increase. The actress's exotic name and the character's aristocratic name both gained mainstream legitimacy. When you're famous, all your names become acceptable.
