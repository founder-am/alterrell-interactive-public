The Malcolm X Effect

Denzel won Best Actor in 2001 for "Training Day"—only the second Black man ever to win Lead Actor. But his name peaked in 1992, the year of "Malcolm X." Spike Lee's epic was culturally massive, especially in Black communities. It was political, powerful, necessary. For Black audiences, portraying Malcolm X carried more weight than an Oscar for playing a corrupt cop.
