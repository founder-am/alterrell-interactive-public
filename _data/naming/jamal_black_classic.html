JAMAL

Peak: 1,246 babies (1993) • Still strong in 2019 (255 babies)
Post-segregation establishment • Not celebrity-driven • Arabic for "beauty"

What Makes a Black Classic?

Jamal emerged post-Civil Rights Act (1964), as Black parents asserted naming autonomy. The name grew steadily from 284 babies (1970) to 1,246 (1993)—not because of a celebrity, but because of a cultural movement.

Influenced by Nation of Islam, Black Power, and the embrace of African/Arabic heritage, "Jamal" became a Black Classic: established within the community, sustained for decades, transcending any single famous bearer.

Still 255 babies in 2019—the name outlasted its era because it represented something deeper than fame. It represented identity, pride, resistance.
