Fresh Prince of Bel-Air • 1990–1996

Alfonso Ribeiro (actor, Spanish/Portuguese name) played Carlton Banks (character, preppy WASP name)

Alfonso Ribeiro brought Carlton Banks to life on The Fresh Prince of Bel-Air starting in 1990. The show ran for six seasons, making Carlton one of TV's most iconic characters—the sweater-vest-wearing, Tom Jones-loving, preppy foil to Will Smith's street-smart charm.

But here's the question: Did audiences name their kids after the actor (Alfonso—a distinctive Spanish/Portuguese name) or the character (Carlton—a traditional upper-class name)? The data tells a fascinating story.

Alfonso peaked in 1993 at 544 babies—up from a pre-1990 average of 265. Meanwhile, Carlton actually peaked decades earlier in 1962 (815) and declined throughout the show's run. The actor's ethnic name gained legitimacy. The character's preppy name did not.
