A name is not "ghetto" until you're popular.

MARIAH

Carey • Debut June 1990

For thirty years, the name Mariah averaged fewer than 400 babies annually. It wasn't trendy. It wasn't popular. It simply existed in obscurity.

Then came June 12, 1990. A 20-year-old with a five-octave range released her self-titled debut. "Vision of Love" topped charts. The whistle notes became legendary. And suddenly, Mariah meant something.

By 1992, the name exploded to 5,192 babies. The peak came in 1996 with 5,446—right after Daydream dominated radio worldwide.

Career Timeline

1990
Mariah Carey (Debut Album)
Released June 12, 1990. Four consecutive #1 singles: "Vision of Love," "Love Takes Time," "Someday," "I Don't Wanna Cry." Established her signature whistle register.
1991
Emotions
Grammy Award: Best New Artist. Title track "Emotions" became her fifth consecutive #1, showcasing unprecedented vocal range.
1993
Music Box
Best-selling album of her career: 28 million copies worldwide. "Hero" and "Dreamlover" dominated charts. Solidified mainstream cultural dominance.
1994
Merry Christmas
Featured "All I Want for Christmas Is You"—now a perennial holiday standard and one of the best-selling singles of all time.
1995
Daydream
"One Sweet Day" (with Boyz II Men) held #1 for 16 consecutive weeks— a record at the time. "Fantasy" pioneered hip-hop/pop crossover collaborations.
1997
Butterfly
Marked artistic evolution and personal liberation. "Honey" became her third single to debut at #1. Critical and commercial success confirmed longevity.
1999
Rainbow
Featured "Heartbreaker" with Jay-Z. Continued to push creative boundaries while maintaining chart dominance into the new millennium.
