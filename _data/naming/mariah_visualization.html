Key Observations

Before Mariah Carey's debut album in June 1990, the name "Mariah" was given to fewer than 400 babies per year on average.

Following her breakthrough with "Vision of Love" and subsequent chart dominance, the name experienced a dramatic surge. The peak came in 1996 — right after the release of Daydream (1995) which featured mega-hits like "Fantasy" and "One Sweet Day."

This 30-year baseline before her debut proves this wasn't a coincidental trend or pre-existing name popularity — it was directly tied to her success as a cultural icon.
