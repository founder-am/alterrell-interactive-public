The Draft Year Phenomenon

Before 1996, the name "Kobe" barely existed—an average of 7 babies per year, mostly unrelated to basketball. Then Kobe Bryant was drafted #13 overall by the Los Angeles Lakers in 1996, straight out of high school.

Immediate impact: 87 babies in 1996 (draft year). Parents didn't wait—they saw a teenage prodigy and claimed the name instantly. By 1998 (rookie breakout), it hit 1,104. By 2001 (first championship), 1,552—the peak.

This pattern is unique to sports: names spike the moment a player enters professional leagues, before they've proven themselves. Parents are betting on potential, not achievements. "Kobe" became aspirational before Kobe was legendary.
