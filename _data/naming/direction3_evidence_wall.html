MARIAH

Carey • Debut Album June 1990

For thirty years, the name Mariah was given to fewer than 400 babies per year on average. It wasn't trendy. It wasn't popular. It simply existed.

Then came June 12, 1990. A 20-year-old singer released her self-titled debut album. "Vision of Love" topped the charts. The whistle notes became legendary. And suddenly, Mariah meant something.

By 1992—just two years later—the name exploded to 5,192 babies. The peak came in 1996 with 5,446, right after Daydream and "One Sweet Day" dominated radio.

This is the pattern: A name is not "ghetto" until you're popular. Cultural legitimacy follows fame. Mainstream acceptance follows success.
