Black Classics

GENDER DIVIDE

Male Names Sustained • Female Names Faded

A striking pattern emerges when we compare male vs. female Black Classic names from the 1970s. Male names sustained or grew: Malik exploded to 3,272 index, Darius climbed to 713, Kareem stayed stable near 100.

Meanwhile, female names collapsed. Latoya peaked at 420 index (1984), now sits at 1. Tamika peaked in 1975, essentially extinct by 2005. Keisha, Ebony, Tanisha—all faded.

Why? Female names in the 1970s were often more innovative, distinctive. By the 2000s, they felt dated. Male names—often Arabic/Islamic in origin—had deeper cultural/religious roots that transcended generational taste shifts.
