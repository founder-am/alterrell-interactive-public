## KEY INSIGHTS DOCUMENTED

### **Oscar Winners Pattern:**
- Halle Berry: Historic first = massive spike
- Denzel Washington: Peaked during Malcolm X (1992), NOT Oscar (2001)
- Viola Davis: Sustained TV + Oscar = gradual growth
- **Lesson:** Cultural significance > institutional validation

### **Arab/Muslim Names Context:**
- Nation of Islam (1930s-1950s) → Black Power (1960s-1970s) → Hip-Hop (1980s-1990s)
- Cultural adoption ≠ religious practice
- Names as political/identity statement, not just immigration
- Key names: Jamal, Malik, Aaliyah, Khadijah, Latifah, Kareem

### **Sitcom "Waiting to See" Pattern:**
- Parents don't name immediately at show debut
- They watch 2-5 years to validate character/actor
- Tatyana Ali: Show ended 1996, peaked 1999 (3-year lag)
- Jada Pinkett Smith: A Different World 1991, peaked 2005 (14-year lag)

### **Counterexamples Identified:**
- **Tisha Campbell:** Peaked 1971 (before career), declined during Martin
- **Venus Williams:** Name was more popular in 1970s than during her career
- **Lesson:** Even beloved characters can't reverse generational decline
