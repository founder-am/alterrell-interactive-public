## SECTION 1: HALLE BERRY

### Header: 🏆 The Historic First

**[EMBED: halle_berry_oscar.html OR inline image]**

Before the 1990s, "Halle" was rare—around 17 babies per year. Then Halle Berry's career took off:
- **1992:** "Boomerang" with Eddie Murphy
- **1999:** "Introducing Dorothy Dandridge" (Emmy win)
- **2000:** "X-Men" as Storm

By 2000, the name hit 639 babies. A 40× increase, but not yet explosive.

### The Oscar Moment
Then March 2002 happened. Halle Berry won Best Actress for "Monster's Ball." She's the first Black woman **ever** to win in that category. The speech is emotional, historic:

> "This moment is so much bigger than me... This is for every nameless, faceless woman of color that now has a chance because this door tonight has been opened."

### The Data Response
**2002: 971 babies named Halle.**

The peak year. A 55× increase from baseline.

## SECTION 2: DENZEL - THE SURPRISE

### Header: 🎭 The Malcolm X Mystery

**[EMBED: denzel_washington.html OR inline image]**

Denzel Washington complicates the story.

In 2001, Denzel won Best Actor for "Training Day"—only the second Black man ever to win Lead Actor, after Sidney Poitier in 1963. This should create a massive spike, right?

**Wrong.**

In 2001, only **196 babies** were named Denzel. That's actually *below* the previous year.

### The Real Peak
But look at 1992: **767 babies**—four times higher than the Oscar year.

What happened in 1992?

**"Malcolm X."**

### Analysis
Spike Lee's biographical epic. Culturally massive, especially in Black communities. Political, powerful, necessary.

### Pull Quote
> "For Black audiences, cultural significance can matter MORE than Oscar validation. Denzel's name spiked from portraying Malcolm X, not from winning an Oscar for playing a corrupt cop."

## SECTION 3: VIOLA - THE ALTERNATIVE MODEL

### Header: 📺 Sustained Visibility

**[EMBED: viola_davis.html OR inline image]**

Viola Davis shows us a third pattern: **sustained television + awards = gradual legitimacy.**

Timeline:
- **2014:** "How to Get Away with Murder" debuts (194 babies)
- **2015:** Emmy win, viral acceptance speech
- **2016:** First Oscar for "Fences" (223 babies)
- **2019:** Peak at 233 babies

### The Pattern
This isn't a single historic spike like Halle. This is **consistency over time**.

Every year on TV, Viola is in millions of homes. The Emmy validates. The Oscar cements. And the name grows steadily, not explosively.

## SECTION 4: WHAT THE OSCARS REVEAL

### Header: 🎬 Patterns of Legitimacy

What do the Oscars reveal about naming legitimacy?

**ONE: Historic firsts create the biggest spikes.**  
Halle Berry's barrier-breaking generated 55× growth. Denzel's second-ever win didn't have the same symbolic power.

**TWO: Cultural significance can outweigh institutional validation.**  
For Black audiences, what the work represents matters. Malcolm X carried more naming weight than Training Day—even with an Oscar attached.

**THREE: Sustained visibility works differently.**  
Viola Davis shows that TV consistency + awards can create gradual, stable growth. Not explosive, but real and lasting.
