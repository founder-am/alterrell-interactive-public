THE GREAT DECLINE

What 1970s Parents Chose ≠ What 2000s Parents Chose

Three names that defined Black naming in the 1970s: Latoya, Tamika, Keisha. Between them, over 8,000 babies at peak. Distinctive, cultural, unapologetically Black.

By 2019, all three had collapsed. Latoya fell 99.7% from peak. Tamika and Keisha became essentially extinct. What happened?

These weren't celebrity-driven names—they were generational choices. And the next generation rejected them. What felt fresh and defiant in 1975 felt dated by 2005. The names their parents chose to assert Black identity became markers of a specific era their children wanted to move past.
