The Generational Shift

Why did these names fade? Not because they were tied to celebrities who fell from grace. These were generational choices—names that felt revolutionary in 1975 felt dated by 2005.

The parents of the 1970s were Baby Boomers and early Gen X—first generation post-segregation, asserting Black identity through names. Their millennial children, naming babies in the 2000s, had different contexts.

By the 2000s, Blackness was mainstream differently. Hip-hop was global. Obama was approaching presidency. The names that represented resistance in 1975 represented a past era in 2005. Naming patterns are generational—what defines you is what you reject from your parents.
