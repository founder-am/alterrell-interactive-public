## PIECE CONTEXT

The BTU Miniseries Cluster (Cluster C) covers scripted limited series, miniseries, and prestige TV movies with majority-Black casts. These titles do not meet the season minimum used in Clusters A and B, so they use a different eligibility gate: **awards validation only**.

This cluster is particularly important for connecting Founders-era actors (Roots, 1977) to the broader network and for recognizing prestige work that doesn't fit the multi-season format.

---

## ELIGIBILITY RULES (LOCKED)

### No season minimum for this cluster.

An entry qualifies for Cluster C if it meets ALL of the following:
- Scripted television (not film, not documentary, not reality)
- 80%+ Black cast OR majority-Black subject matter with majority-Black credited cast
- **At minimum one** of the following award validations:
  - Emmy nomination or win (any category)
  - Golden Globe nomination or win
  - NAACP Image Award nomination or win
  - Peabody Award

### OD-10 hard line (applies platform-wide)
A show qualifies as Primary if EITHER of the following is met:
- 80%+ of the cast is Black, OR
- There are two leads and at least one lead is Black

If neither condition is met, individual actors with BTU-qualifying credits are still part of the BTU network — but the show itself does not earn a Primary node.

### Actor eligibility within Cluster C
An actor earns a BTU node via Cluster C if they are **main or lead cast** in a qualifying title. A Cluster C credit alone is sufficient for a node — no additional show requirement.

Cluster C nodes connect to Cluster A and B nodes via **verified shared cast appearances in other shows**. The miniseries credit is the entry point; the connections are built from their broader career.
