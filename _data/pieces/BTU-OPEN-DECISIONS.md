## CROSS-CLUSTER DECISIONS (affect multiple briefs)

**OD-01 — Non-Black creator, majority-Black cast**
Clusters affected: B (Drama), C (Miniseries)
Question: Does a show with a non-Black creator qualify as a BTU Primary show if it has an 80%+ Black cast?
Specific cases: The Wire (David Simon), The Corner (David Simon), Treme (David Simon), The White Shadow (Bruce Paltrow)
Options:
- A) Creator race is irrelevant; 80% Black cast is the only gate → The Wire, The Corner, Treme all qualify Primary
- B) Creator must be Black for Primary; mixed-creator majority-Black shows qualify Secondary → Wire goes Secondary
- C) Creator race is irrelevant but show must be set in Black community/culture, not just cast → Wire and Corner qualify; White Shadow borderline
**Status: RESOLVED — 2026-06-03**
**Ruling: Option C.** Creator race is irrelevant. Gate is cultural centering: the show must be set in and centered on Black community and culture. The Wire, The Corner, and Treme all qualify as Primary. The White Shadow is borderline — apply the NAACP tiebreaker or designate Secondary pending further review.

## CLUSTER C (MINISERIES) DECISIONS

**OD-10 — Show-level eligibility rule (hard line)**
Clusters affected: C (Miniseries), and applied platform-wide
Question: What is the hard eligibility rule for a show's Primary designation when cast composition is uncertain?
**Status: RESOLVED — 2026-06-03**
**Ruling (hard line, applies platform-wide):** A show qualifies as Primary if EITHER of the following is met:
- 80%+ of the cast is Black, OR
- There are two leads and at least one lead is Black

If neither condition is met, individual actors with BTU-qualifying credits are still part of the BTU network — but the show itself does not earn a Primary node.

**Applied to Seven Seconds (OD-10):** Regina King's Emmy win confirms her as a BTU node regardless. The show's Primary/Secondary designation depends on cast verification against the hard line above. Research pass required.
