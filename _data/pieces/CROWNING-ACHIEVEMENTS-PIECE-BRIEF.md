## EDITORIAL PREMISE

A data journalism card series honoring Black women who shaped American music. Each artist gets a set of 4 cards. The cards are typographic and data-forward — no photos. The design register is collectible card: portrait format, dark background, bold hierarchy.

**Tagline (working):** *She built the stage everyone else performs on.*

**Data as Dignity framing:** Data reveals what the industry recognized — and what it failed to recognize. Grammy gaps, Rock Hall absences, and crossover records that rewrote commercial music are all part of the story. The cards do not judge individuals. They show the architecture.

## CARD ARCHITECTURE — 4 CARDS PER ARTIST (Tiers 1 & 2)

All four cards share identical dimensions and base layout. The content zone changes per card type. Card number and type are labeled in the header on every card.

### Card 1 — Stats
**Purpose:** Quantitative achievement data. Charts, peaks, record-setting firsts.
**Layout:**
- Header block: artist name (large) + honorific (accent color, italic)
- Primary stat: one dominant number, large — the most record-setting verifiable figure for this artist
- Secondary stats: 2–3 supporting numbers in a row beneath
- Visual: Horizontal bar chart — decade breakdown of Hot 100 entries (or R&B entries for Foundation-tier artists where Hot 100 is thin)
- Footer: card number indicator

**Data rules:**
- All stats sourced from verified research document only
- No comparative framing within a racial group ("more than any Black woman" is retired)
- Acceptable phrasing: "most for any solo artist," "most for any woman," "first ever"
- Grammy wins = competitive wins only; special awards listed separately on Card 4
- If decade bar chart data is unavailable for an artist, replace with alternative visual treatment (confirmed in research doc per artist)

### Card 2 — The Reign
**Purpose:** 5–6 milestone moments in timeline format. The career arc.
**Layout:**
- Header block: artist name + honorific (same as Card 1)
- Small visual anchor at top of timeline zone: era label, decade marker, or decorative typographic element — prevents empty space on shorter reigns
- Timeline: vertical, 5–6 entries, each with year (DM Mono) and milestone text (DM Sans)
- Timeline line: accent color, runs full height of entries
- Footer: card number indicator

**Copy rules:**
- Milestones must be verified — year and event confirmed in research document
- Milestone text is short (one line max) — this is a timeline, not prose
- Suggested milestone structure: debut · first #1 · peak cultural moment · pivot/reinvention (if applicable) · final major milestone (award, induction, or comeback)

### Card 3 — Infrastructure
**Purpose:** The one structural thing this artist changed for everyone who came after.
**Layout:**
- Header block: artist name + honorific
- Pull quote: dominant typographic element — large, spans most of the card. This is editorial framing, NOT an attributed real quote. AMA writes this in voice pass.
- Infrastructure pills: 2–4 category tags in DM Mono, lowercase, bordered — e.g. `crossover architecture` · `label power` · `tour economy`
- Supporting line: one sentence of prose. Minimal. The pull quote does the work.
- Footer: card number indicator

**Copy rules:**
- Pull quote is original editorial framing — it must not be attributed to a real person
- Infrastructure claim must be factually grounded in research document
- Pills reflect what is documented, not assumed
- No invented percentages — if a crossover rate or stat is cited, it must have a named source

### Card 4 — Bio
**Purpose:** Who she is. Factual only. The credential card.
**Layout:**
- Header block: artist name + honorific
- Honorific line: full honorific text, accent color
- Bio block: birthplace/city association, active years
- Awards block: Grammy wins (competitive) · Grammy nominations · Grammy special awards (Lifetime Achievement, Hall of Fame inductions) — each on its own line
- Other major awards: Oscar nominations/wins · Tony · Kennedy Center Honors · Presidential Medal of Freedom · Rock & Roll Hall of Fame induction year(s) · Songwriters Hall of Fame · other Hall of Fame inductions
- One-sentence legacy line at bottom
- Footer: card number indicator

**Data rules:**
- Grammy count = competitive wins only (stated as "X Grammy wins")
- Special Grammy awards listed separately and labeled
- Rock Hall induction: include year and category (Performer / Musical Excellence / Musical Influence) — these are not equivalent and must not be conflated
- All figures from verified research document only

## SPECIAL EDITION CARD — 1 CARD PER ARTIST

Single card per artist. Smaller data footprint. The card's power comes from framing.

**Layout:**
- Artist name (large)
- Honorific or title
- Era band: e.g. `1920s–1950s` in DM Mono
- One dominant framing line (editorial, not attributed quote): what did she build before there were charts to measure it?
- What exists because of her: 2–3 short lines
- Awards/recognition: Grammy Lifetime Achievement (year) · Rock Hall (year) · other
- Note where recognition was absent — the gap is part of the card
