## PIECE CONTEXT

The BTU Soap Opera Cluster (Cluster D) is a **separate visual cluster** within the BTU network. Soaps operate on different logic than scripted dramas and sitcoms — actors often appear for decades on a single show, episode counts reach into the thousands, and the same actors migrate across multiple soap properties as shows get cancelled and casts disperse.

---

## ELIGIBILITY RULES (LOCKED)

### Primary Tier
An actor qualifies for the BTU Soap Cluster Primary Tier if they meet either:
- Main or recurring cast on **1 soap opera for 3+ seasons**, OR
- Recurring cast on **2+ soap operas** regardless of season count

"Soap opera" = daily or weekly serialized drama, produced for daytime or primetime, running in ongoing open-ended format.

### NAACP Tiebreaker
NAACP Image Award nomination or win for a soap role is sufficient to confirm inclusion for borderline actors. The NAACP Image Awards ran a dedicated Daytime Drama category from 1993–2015 (category retired after 2015). This is the primary validation source for this cluster.

### What does NOT qualify
- Reality programming
- Scripted primetime dramas with soap-like serialization (Power, Empire) — those belong in Cluster B
- Guest appearances under 3 episodes

## GENERATIONS (NBC, 1989–1991) — HISTORICAL ANCHOR
*OD-12 ruling: Show documented as historical note. Individual actors with NAACP nominations qualify as BTU nodes.*

**Basic facts:** Aired March 27, 1989 – January 25, 1991. 470 episodes. Half-hour daily format. NBC.

**Historical significance:** First U.S. soap opera to feature a Black American family from inception. Set in Chicago. Centered on the Marshall family (Black, owners of an ice cream empire) and the Whitmore family (white). Not cancelled for cause — low ratings in a competitive daytime slot.

## STRUCTURAL SUMMARY FOR BUILD

**Load-bearing center:** Debbi Morgan. Eight confirmed soap credits across four decades. Connects AMC, Generations, Port Charles, B&B, and Y&R.

**Dominant institutional anchor:** The Young and the Restless (CBS). Owns the NAACP Daytime Drama category almost entirely 1993–2015. The Winters-Barber family (Neil, Dru, Malcolm, Olivia, Devon, Lily) is the largest Black recurring family unit in daytime history.

**Secondary anchor:** All My Children (ABC). The Jesse-and-Angie supercouple era (1981–1990) is the first major Black storyline in daytime history. AMC is the origin show for Morgan, Williams, Pinkins, Cobb, Jordan, and Smith Jr.

**Historical anchor:** Generations (NBC, 1989–1991). First soap with a Black family from inception. Not a network node per OD-12 ruling — documented here as historical record. Already connected to the cluster through St. John, Morgan, Fox, and Williams.

**Suggested visual treatment:** Cluster D rendered as a distinct horizontal band or separate constellation. Y&R nodes form the dense center mass. AMC nodes form a secondary cluster connected to Y&R via the Morgan/Williams/St. John shared-actor edges. Crossover nodes (Rowell → Diagnosis Murder, James → Family Matters) carry edges to Clusters A and B.
