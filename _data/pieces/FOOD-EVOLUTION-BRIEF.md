The same food, different product
Thesis: When a boomer complains "I ate plenty of bread and I'm fine," the bread they remember doesn't exist anymore. Every staple food is radically more synthetic than its 1960s counterpart — the same name on the box, but 3-4x the ingredients inside. Our dietary health burden is less about personal choice and more about a chemical arms race we didn't ask for.
