## PIECE CONTEXT

The BTU Drama Cluster (Cluster B) documents Black TV actors in scripted hour-long dramas. It is a **separate visual cluster** from the sitcom cluster — same network diagram, distinct visual zone. Think Marvel and DC: separate canons, both canonical, occasional crossover nodes.

Crossover nodes (actors who qualify in both clusters) get a special marker and appear in both clusters with a connecting edge.

---

## ELIGIBILITY RULES (LOCKED)

### Primary Tier (80%+ Black cast)
An actor qualifies for the BTU Drama Primary Tier if they meet either:
- Main or recurring cast on **1 Black drama for 3+ seasons**, OR
- Recurring cast on **2+ Black dramas** regardless of season count

"Black drama" = scripted hour-long drama with 80%+ Black cast.

### Secondary Tier (Black lead, mixed cast)
An actor qualifies for Secondary Tier if:
- They were the **Black lead** on a drama with a mixed cast
- The show ran **3+ seasons**
- The actor received an **Emmy nomination or NAACP Image Award nomination** in a lead or supporting drama category for that role

### NAACP Tiebreaker
NAACP Image Award nomination or win for that role is sufficient to confirm inclusion for borderline actors.

### What does NOT qualify
- Reality television
- Sketch comedy, variety, talk
- Guest appearances under 3 episodes on a single show
- Film credits

## OPEN DECISIONS (Must resolve before research pass closes)

1. **The Wire / non-Black-created majority-Black shows** — The Wire is created by David Simon (white), who also created The Corner and Treme. All three have majority-Black casts and Black cultural subject matter. Does creator race disqualify a show with an 80%+ Black cast? If The Wire is in, it becomes the drama cluster's single biggest hub. If it's out, Clarke Peters and Wendell Pierce still qualify via The Corner and Treme. **RULING NEEDED.**

2. **New York Undercover (1994–1998)** — First police drama with two leads of color (Malik Yoba is Black; Michael DeLorenzo is Latino). The show is majority non-white but not majority-Black. Does it qualify as Primary, Secondary, or not at all? **RULING NEEDED.**

3. **The Shonda Rhimes universe** — Scandal and HTGAWM are confirmed Secondary tier. But the casts of those shows overlap heavily with other BTU-eligible actors. Do Kerry Washington and Viola Davis get Secondary nodes that connect to Primary nodes via verified cast credits (e.g., Khandi Alexander appeared on Scandal as Maya Lewis)? **Recommend: Yes — this is how Khandi Alexander bridges from Drama Primary to Drama Secondary cluster.**

4. **Under One Roof (1995, CBS)** — 6-episode run, cancelled. James Earl Jones lead. Too short for BTU threshold. Flag as **historical note only** — first Black-majority drama on CBS network. Not eligible.

5. **City of Angels (2000, CBS)** — Only 2 seasons. Below 3-season threshold. **Secondary Tier only via Black lead rule** (Blair Underwood). Notable as network TV's first Black-majority medical drama — include as historical anchor with asterisk.
