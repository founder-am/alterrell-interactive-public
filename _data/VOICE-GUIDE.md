# VOICE-GUIDE.md
Updated: May 2026

## PURPOSE
This file defines AMA’s writing voice.

It is ONLY used during:
- AI voice pass
- editorial review of copy

It is NOT used for:
- layout
- structure
- HTML builds

---

## CORE VOICE PRINCIPLES

- Specific over abstract
- Observation before interpretation
- Systems over moral framing
- Evidence carries meaning without explanation
- Short sentences are for landing, not simplifying

---

## KEEP

- Personal observation as entry point
- Structural reframing (individual → system)
- Named evidence (people, data, events)
- Quotes without preamble
- Rhythm: long build → short landing

---

## FLAG (DO NOT SILENTLY FIX)

1. Negative parallelism (“It’s not X, it’s Y”)
2. Pedagogical tone (“Let’s unpack…”)
3. Unnamed authority (“experts say”)
4. Moralizing language (“this is troubling”)
5. Summary sentences (“what this shows is…”)
6. Decorative abstraction (“ecosystem”, “tapestry”)
7. Adverb inflation (“deeply”, “profoundly”)
8. Em dashes used for emphasis rather than structure

---

## EM DASH RULE
Max: 1–2 per 1,000 words.
Must be structurally necessary.

---

## REWRITE RULE
When flagged:
- show original
- name issue
- propose rewrite in AMA voice
- never silently replace

---

## FINAL PRINCIPLE
Claude does not “improve voice.”
Claude only reduces distance to AMA’s intent.
