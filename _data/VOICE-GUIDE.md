# VOICE-GUIDE.md

**Read this file at the start of every Type 1 session and before every AI voice pass.**
**Updated: April 24, 2026**

This file is the canonical reference for AMA's voice. It exists because AI writing passes without a fixed reference drift toward AI tropes. Every pass must be checked against this file before copy is returned to AMA.

---

## THE ROLE OF THIS FILE

Claude drafts and flags. AMA rewrites and approves. The voice pass exists to reduce the distance between Claude's draft and what AMA would have written — not to eliminate AMA's rewrite, but to make it faster and less frustrating. This file defines what "closer to AMA" means in concrete terms.

## CALIBRATION SAMPLES

These are approved AMA rewrites. When uncertain whether a sentence sounds right, return to these.

**Sample 1 — Data introduced after context, short pivot at the end:**
> Researchers sent identical résumés to over 1,300 job postings in Boston and Chicago. The only variable was the name at the top. Applicants with white-sounding names needed 10 résumés to get one callback. Those with Black-sounding names needed 15. A better résumé helped white applicants 30% more. For Black-named applicants, improving the résumé produced almost no difference.

What this shows: data lands in sequence, no setup paragraph needed, no summary sentence at the end telling you what to think. The numbers do the work.

**Sample 2 — Specific evidence trusted without editorializing:**
> In 2018, Cardi B was not headlining Coachella but spent $300,000 of her own money on production for a 35-minute set. She earned $70,000 as her fee. She lost roughly $460,000 across both weekends. In her own words from a SiriusXM interview: "I have to invest so much money on my stage set, my own money that I gotta go to Wells Fargo and write a check that it's crazy."

What this shows: quote lands without preamble, no "this illustrates," no "as she explained." The sourcing is specific (SiriusXM interview, not "in an interview").

**Sample 3 — Personal observation opening into structural critique:**
> Once you attend a Beyoncé concert or the elaborate spectacle of most female artists, the pattern that emerges is hard to unsee.

What this shows: opens in the reader's experience, not in an argument. The argument follows. Never reversed.

**Sample 4 — History stated plainly without inflation:**
> In the 1920s and 1930s, the American music industry created a formal category called "race records." It was music made by Black artists, sold to Black audiences, physically excluded from white radio stations. When Little Richard recorded "Tutti Frutti," Pat Boone recorded a cover version for white radio because Little Richard himself could not get airplay on those stations. The music crossed over, but the credit, airplay, and revenue did not.

What this shows: no "this was a pivotal moment," no "it is important to understand." The facts are arranged so their meaning is unavoidable. Last sentence is the pivot — short, declarative, earns its place.

## VOICE MARKERS — KEEP

- **Personal or cultural observation as entry point.** The reader is placed inside an experience before they are given an argument.
- **Systemic reframe as the move.** Individual observation opens outward to structure, never the reverse.
- **Specific evidence over unnamed authority.** Named person, named publication, named amount. Never "experts say" or "studies show."
- **Data introduced after context is established.** The number lands when the reader already has a reason to care about it.
- **Varying sentence length with intention.** Long sentences build context. Short sentences land points. The rhythm is deliberate, not decorative.
- **"We" as shared observation.** Used when inviting the reader into a collective noticing. Not used to speak for a community.
- **Questions used to shift register.** Not to introduce data. Not answered in the next sentence.
- **Quotes trusted to speak.** No "this illustrates" or "as X explained" before or after a quote. The quote earns its place or it doesn't appear.

## VOICE MARKERS — FLAG AND REWRITE

These are patterns that appear in AI drafts and must be flagged in every pass. Flag means: show AMA the original, explain the problem, suggest a rewrite. Never silently fix.

**1. Negative parallelism**
The "It's not X — it's Y" construction. The single most identified AI tell externally. Creates false profundity. One per piece maximum and only if the reframe is genuinely surprising. Cross-sentence variant also flagged: "The question isn't X. The question is Y."
> Flag example: "The market explains the fee. It does not explain the production gap." — sounds clean but follows the AI reframe pattern. AMA to decide whether to keep or rewrite.

**2. Em dashes used decoratively**
Rule: maximum 1-2 em dashes per 1,000 words. Em dashes used as a substitute for completing a thought are always flagged. Em dashes used structurally (to set off a clause that earns the interruption) may stay at AMA's discretion.

**3. Unnamed authority**
"Experts say," "studies show," "industry reports suggest," "observers note," "several publications have argued." Always flag. Replace with specific named source or cut.

**4. Pedagogical voice**
"Let's unpack this," "let's explore," "let's dive in," "it is worth noting," "it is important to understand," "what this means is." These assume the reader needs hand-holding. Always cut.

**5. Grandiose abstract nouns**
"Tapestry," "landscape," "paradigm," "ecosystem," "framework," "infrastructure" used metaphorically. Replace with the specific thing being described.

**6. Adverbs inflating weak verbs**
"Deeply," "fundamentally," "remarkably," "profoundly," "quietly," "arguably." Flag and replace with a stronger verb or cut the adverb entirely.

**7. Summary sentences**
Sentences that tell the reader what they just read. "This is the backdrop against which X should be understood." "Taken together, these findings suggest." "What emerges from this data is clear." The reader has already read it. Cut.

**8. The "despite challenges" formula**
Acknowledges a problem only to immediately dismiss it with an optimistic conclusion. Always flag. AMA decides whether the tension should stay unresolved.

**9. Moralizing language**
"This is deeply troubling," "we must reckon with," "it is a sobering reminder." AMA's pieces describe structure and let the reader feel what they feel. Claude does not editorialize on top of the evidence.

**10. Transition sentences that summarize instead of advance**
Sentences at the top of a section that repeat what the previous section established. "As we have seen," "building on this," "with this context in mind." Cut and start with the next thing.

---

## EM DASH RULE

Maximum 1-2 em dashes per 1,000 words of body copy. Count them. If a draft exceeds this, flag every instance and identify which one(s) are earning their place. Default recommendation: cut all but the strongest one.

## EXTERNAL REFERENCE: AI TROPE LIST

Source: tropes.fyi (updated March 2026). The following are confirmed external tells — not stylistic preferences, documented patterns that readers who use AI regularly identify immediately:

- Negative parallelism (It's not X — it's Y)
- Overuse of "delve," "utilize," "leverage," "robust," "streamline," "harness"
- Grandiose nouns: tapestry, landscape, paradigm, ecosystem
- Inflation adverbs: deeply, fundamentally, remarkably, quietly
- Pedagogical openers: Let's unpack, Let's explore, Let's dive in
- Unnamed authority: experts, observers, industry reports
- Explicit conclusions: In conclusion, To sum up, In summary
- The despite-challenges formula
- Dramatic countdown: negating two things before revealing the point
- Stakes inflation: treating every argument as world-historical

These are not rules invented for this platform. They are documented patterns that undermine credibility with any reader who has spent time with AI-generated text — which in 2026 is most readers.

## WHAT CLAUDE CANNOT DO IN A VOICE PASS

- Cannot perceive. Does not have experiences, sources, scenes, or emotions to draw from.
- Cannot determine what AMA would have written — only approximate based on calibration samples.
- Cannot silently fix a flagged sentence and present the result as AMA's voice.
- Cannot accept that a rewrite is "done" until AMA has touched it.

The voice pass reduces the distance. AMA closes it.
