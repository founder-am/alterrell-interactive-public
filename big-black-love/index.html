Methodology & Sources

This piece draws on community-generated poll data from a TikTok post (@alterrell) and overlays it with publicly available socioeconomic data. The poll is not a representative sample. Cities were reported voluntarily by respondents from the full comment thread. n≈100 strict partner mentions from approximately 85 unique commenters across 25+ cities. Cards show top 10-11 cities; remaining cities acknowledged in copy. Atlanta and Chicago are near-tied at the top; framing as “tied” is defensible given the methodology. Video: 4K+ views, 562 likes, 174 comments. Previous tally (n=77, April 26) superseded by full-thread count.

  • Community poll data: TikTok (@alterrell), May 2026. n≈100 strict city-mentions from ~85 unique commenters. Cities reported voluntarily by respondents identifying as bigger Black gay men who found a Black partner. Not statistically representative.
  • LGBT population by metro area: Conron, K.J., Luhur, W., & Goldberg, S.K. “Estimated number of US LGBT adults in Large Metropolitan Statistical Areas (MSA).” Williams Institute, UCLA, March 2021. Based on Gallup Daily Tracking Survey 2012-2017 applied to 2017 Census ACS estimates. All-LGBT (not gay men specifically, not race-adjusted). Key figures: NYC-Newark-Jersey City MSA 706,000 (4.5%); ATL-Sandy Springs-Alpharetta MSA 194,000 (4.6%). NYC/ATL ratio: 3.6×.
  • Obesity prevalence by race and state: CDC BRFSS 2022-2024, “Prevalence of Obesity Based on Self-Reported Weight and Height Among Non-Hispanic Black Adults / Non-Hispanic White Adults, by State and Territory.” Three-year pooled data, self-reported BMI ≥30. State-level only (not city-level) — used as a geographic proxy for poll cities. PA and LA excluded (insufficient data). Card framing: “above-average BMI” not “obesity.” Point spread format: Black adult rate minus White adult rate.
  • Black median household income: U.S. Census Bureau, ACS 2019-2023 5-Year Estimates via Neilsberg Research (2023 inflation-adjusted dollars). Confirmed: DC $60,089; Atlanta (city proper) $47,937. US national Black median: $53,444. Houston, Chicago, NYC, LA: directional figures only — full Census pull recommended before deploy.
  • Black population share by city (M-3): Census ACS 2019-2023. Detroit city ~77% Black; Atlanta city proper ~47% Black.
