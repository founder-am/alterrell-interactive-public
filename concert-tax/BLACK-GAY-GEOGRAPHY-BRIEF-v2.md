## VERIFIED DATA SOURCES

### Williams Institute — LGBT population by metro area
**Source:** Conron, K.J., Luhur, W., & Goldberg, S.K. "Estimated number of US LGBT adults in Large Metropolitan Statistical Areas (MSA)." Williams Institute, UCLA, March 2021.
**Methodology:** Gallup Daily Tracking Survey 2012-2017, applied to 2017 Census ACS population estimates. MSA-level (metro area, not city proper). All-LGBT (not gay men specifically, not race-adjusted).
**Key figures used:**
- New York-Newark-Jersey City MSA: 706,000 (4.5%)
- Los Angeles-Long Beach-Anaheim MSA: 523,000 (5.1%)
- Chicago-Naperville-Elgin MSA: 298,000 (4.1%)
- Washington DC-Arlington-Alexandria MSA: 209,000 (4.5%)
- Philadelphia-Camden-Wilmington MSA: 198,000 (4.2%)
- Atlanta-Sandy Springs-Alpharetta MSA: 194,000 (4.6%)
- Houston-The Woodlands-Sugar Land MSA: 169,000 (3.5%)
- Detroit-Warren-Dearborn MSA: 131,000 (3.9%)
- New Orleans-Metairie MSA: 46,000 (4.7%)
- SF-Oakland-Berkeley MSA: 247,000 (6.7%)
- Dallas-Fort Worth-Arlington MSA: 211,000 (4.0%)

**CRITICAL NOTE:** Gemini transcript figures were wrong. ATL was cited as "22k" — actual Williams figure is 194k (metro). NYC was cited as "415k" — actual is 706k (metro). The 20× ratio was fabricated. Real ratio: NYC/ATL = 3.6×. All cards use verified Williams figures only.

### CDC BRFSS — Obesity prevalence by race and state
**Source:** CDC BRFSS 2022-2024. "Prevalence of Obesity Based on Self-Reported Weight and Height Among Non-Hispanic Black Adults / Non-Hispanic White Adults, by State and Territory."
**Methodology:** Self-reported BMI ≥ 30. Three-year pooled data. State-level (not city-level — used as proxy).
**Key figures used (Black adult rate / White adult rate / gap):**
- DC: 37.3% / 14.7% / +23 pts
- California (LA, Oakland): 38.1% / 25.7% / +12 pts
- Georgia (Atlanta): 44.2% / 33.4% / +11 pts
- Texas (Houston, Dallas): 43.7% / 33.5% / +10 pts
- Illinois (Chicago): 43.1% / 33.6% / +10 pts
- Michigan (Detroit): 43.4% / 35.0% / +8 pts
- New York (NYC): 36.2% / 28.9% / +7 pts

**Card framing:** "Above-average BMI" (not "obesity"). Point spread format. Sub-headline: "One factor among several — not the whole story."
**Pennsylvania (Philly) and Louisiana (NOLA):** Insufficient data in CDC report. Excluded from card.

### Census ACS — Black median household income
**Source:** U.S. Census Bureau, ACS 2019-2023 5-Year Estimates via Neilsberg Research (2023 inflation-adjusted dollars).
**Confirmed figures:**
- Washington DC: $60,089 (Black median household income)
- Atlanta (city proper): $47,937
- US national average (Black): $53,444

**Unconfirmed:** Houston, Chicago, NYC, LA. Gemini figures ($50k, $48k, $45k, $42k) are directional but not pinned to source. Card uses confirmed DC and ATL figures with directional language for others. Full Census pull recommended for Type 2 session.
