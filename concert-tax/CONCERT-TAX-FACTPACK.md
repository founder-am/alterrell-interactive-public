## SECTION 5 — PRODUCTION COST STRUCTURE

### Hardware vs human — the key distinction (locked)

**Hardware production:**
- LED screens, lighting rigs, projections, pyrotechnics, moving set pieces, robots, inflatables
- Cost structure: fixed, rented, modular, reused across tour legs
- Financially stable — pay once, variance minimal per show

**Human production:**
- Dancers, choreographers, stylists, wardrobe teams, rehearsal time, creative directors
- Cost structure: recurring, scales with show count, per-person costs per city
- Multi-country = visa costs per person; touring = per diems + hotels + insurance + liability for each
- Financially volatile — more people = more exposure at every stop

**The core inequality:**
When production complexity increases, one of two things happens: the artist absorbs the cost, or the artist simplifies the show. Female artists are more often expected to absorb the cost. Male artists are more often allowed to simplify the show. Both are paid into similar headline fee ranges, but the internal economics diverge immediately after booking.

## SECTION 8 — THE DUA LIPA ARC (DOCUMENTED FEEDBACK LOOP)

**The sequence:**
1. February 22, 2018: Dua Lipa performs "New Rules" at BRIT Awards
2. YouTube commenter named **Tobias** posts: "I love her lack of energy, go girl give us nothing!"
3. Phrase becomes a meme applied to female artists broadly — also weaponized against Blackpink's Jennie
4. Lipa describes feeling humiliated for **two years**
5. Takes herself off Twitter
6. 2021: Acknowledges meme in Vanity Fair; commits to proving herself
7. 2022: Future Nostalgia Tour — full production with dancers, choreography, costume changes, conceptual staging
8. 2022 Nylon review directly references the original criticism: "It was in 2019 when the internet's first general consensus around Dua Lipa was that she couldn't dance."
9. Critical redemption: reviews describe her as a "powerhouse" and "pop crown" winner

**Why this matters for the piece:**
One YouTube comment cost her two years of confidence and produced a complete production transformation. No equivalent arc — comment → humiliation → production escalation → critical redemption — exists for any male artist in this era. The feedback loop is documented with named source (Tobias), named publication (Vanity Fair), and named artist in her own words.
Source: Know Your Meme; Vanity Fair June 2021; Nylon review 2022; Yahoo Entertainment 2024.

- Stan culture has weaponized the gaps: a bad vocal, an outfit mishap, or something that went wrong in a multi-element production becomes the gotcha

- Dedicated Twitter/X accounts run by faceless figures have made the enforcers of the cycle harder to name and harder to hold accountable
- The feedback loop accelerates: criticism → more production → higher expectations → more criticism risk
